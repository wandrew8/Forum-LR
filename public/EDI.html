EDI: Economic Deplomacy and Integrity

Economic Deplomacy and Integrity

EDI was born out of the prayer of two friends, one Jewish American and the other Serbian, as a response to what to do to help the Balkan region now that the wars in the 90s were over and there was an apparent economic and political need to rebuild the nations of Southeast Europe.

The solution that came was to apply Jesusâ€™s life and principles to change our nations in the fields of economics, politics, and economic relations between ethnic groups and nations. Principles like integrity, moral leadership, service to the community, a focus on the poorest and their needs, faith and transparency can help combat corruption, selfishness, youth hopelessness, debilitating unemployment, and abject poverty. Our hope was and is that when these experiences and principles are applied, they will end up changing and developing the region, not just in terms of economic growth, but through the creation of a community of leaders committed to good economics and politics and most importantly, to each other and God.

I believe that that it is significant that we are having the first and foundational EDI conference in Israel. For me personally as a Jew this is a real blessing to be part of it. God wants to bless and deepen our friendship with one another, to encourage us to have and pursue our dreams, passions and calling. I believe that out of this conference you will have seeds of dreams and deepening visions of hope that will greatly expand the kingdom of God in our world.

Yours with open hearts,


Justin Kagin
